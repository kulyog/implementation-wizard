## What You Actually Built

Implementation Wizard is a personal tool that tracks every software project you build through a fixed 24-step AI-assisted process. It lives at a URL only you use. It stores everything in your browser. It requires no subscription, no server, no maintenance team, and no ongoing cost beyond what you already pay for Claude.

It is not glamorous from the outside. But from the inside, it is the operating system for everything you are going to build next.

From this point forward, every finance application you develop — every screener, every CFO dashboard, every automation tool for a small business client — starts with Step 1 in Implementation Wizard, and ends with Step 24. No more half-built ideas left in Notion. No more lost context when you come back to a project after two weeks away. No more wondering which prompt to use and whether you remembered the right sequence.

You will know exactly where you are. Every time.

---

## What the Process Taught You

Building this application using the framework itself — using Implementation Wizard to build Implementation Wizard — was the most honest possible proof of concept.

You learned that the structure works. Separating the thinking (Steps 1–7) from the building (Steps 8–14) from the verification (Steps 15–20) is not bureaucracy. It is how you avoid building the wrong thing correctly, or the right thing badly. The audit caught real gaps. The re-audit confirmed they were fixed. The documentation forced clarity about what you actually built and why.

You also learned where the friction is. The prompts are still placeholders — that is the outstanding work, and it matters more than any feature. Until real prompts are embedded in every step, the app is a tracker, not an accelerator. Prompt authoring is your next focused session, not an afterthought.

And you learned that the hardest part of building with AI is not the technical capability. It is discipline. Claude can generate nearly anything — the scaffolding, the tests, the components, the documentation. What it cannot do is decide what matters, hold the standard, push back on its own output, or mark a step Complete when it is not truly done. That part was always yours. It still is.

## How to Use What You Now Have

The app is live. Use it from today.

When you start your next project — whatever it is — open Implementation Wizard first. Create the project. Start at Step 1. Write down what you want to build in plain language before you open Claude. That step costs nothing and saves everything.

When you get to the Claude steps, the prompts will guide you. Once the real prompts are authored, this will be close to effortless. Until then, you have the persona descriptions and your experience from building this project as your guide.

A few habits that will make the difference:

**Export at the end of every session.** It takes two seconds. It is the only backup you have. Build it into your closing routine the same way you lock your screen and close VS Code.

**Use the Notes field as your log.** Paste Claude's output. Record the decision you made and why. Note the file path. Leave a breadcrumb for your future self. Three months from now, when a client asks why you made a certain design choice, the answer will be one click away.

**Do not skip the review steps.** Steps 7, 10, 12, 14, 16, and 20 are all marked "Me" — they are the steps where you are the quality gate. Do not rush through them. An hour spent reviewing before building is worth several hours of refactoring after. You have now been through one full cycle. You know this is true.

**Respect the sequence.** The app enforces it for good reason. The decisions you make in Step 2 constrain what is sensible in Step 4. The test cases in Step 6 only hold the build accountable if they were written before the build, not after it. The sequence is not a ceremony — it is a guarantee of quality.

## What Comes Next

There are two immediate tasks before this project is truly complete:

**Author the 24 prompts.** This is the most important thing you can do with the next free Claude session you have. Take the persona descriptions, the expected outputs, and what you know about the process, and draft each prompt. Run them through a single quality-check session. Then embed them in `steps.js`. When that is done, the app will do what it was always meant to do.

**Complete Steps 22–24.** Prepare the release package, confirm the live deployment is working, and schedule your post-launch review for two weeks from now. Step 24 is the reflection step — it exists so you capture what you learned while it is still fresh.

After that — and this is the point of the whole exercise — you start the next project. You open Implementation Wizard, create a new project, and begin again from Step 1. This time, faster. This time, with better prompts. This time, with the confidence of someone who has been through the process once and knows it works.
